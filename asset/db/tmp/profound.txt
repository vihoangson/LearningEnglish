from <a class="w translation" href="  http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/Old-French#Old-French__2" title="Meaning of Old French">Old French</a> <em>profund</em>, from <a class="w translation" href="  http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/Latin#Latin__2" title="Meaning of Latin">Latin</a> <em>profundus</em> 'deep', from <a class="w translation" href="  http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/Latin#Latin__2" title="Meaning of Latin">Latin</a> <em>pro</em> 'before' + <em>fundus</em> 'bottom'. The word was used earliest in the sense 'showing deep insight'.</p><div class="moreInformation"><a class="moreInformationExemples"> More </a><ul class="sentence_dictionary"><li><p class="word_origin"><b>found</b> from Middle English:</p><p class="word_origin">The word found ‘establish’ goes back to Latin <em>fundare</em> ‘to lay a base for’, from <em>fundus</em> ‘bottom, base’, source also of <b>foundation</b> (Late Middle English), <b>founder</b> (Middle English) ‘sink’, and <b>fund</b> (mid 17th century) from a secondary sense of <em>fundus</em> ‘landed property’; and <b>profound</b> (Middle English) ‘deep’. Found ‘melt and mould’ is from French <em>fondre</em> (source of the melted cheese <b>fondue</b> (late 19th century)), from Latin <em>fundere</em> ‘melt, pour’ (found also in <b>fuse</b> (late 16th century)), and dates from the early 16th century.</p></li></ul></div><!-- End of DIV moreInformation--></div><!-- End of DIV senseInnerWrapper--></section><section class="etymology"><div
