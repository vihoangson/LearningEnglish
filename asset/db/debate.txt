subEntry--></dl></section><section class="etymology"><div class="senseInnerWrapper"><h2>Origin</h2><p><span class="dateGroup"> Middle English</span>: via Old French from Latin <em>dis-</em> (expressing reversal) + <em>battere</em> 'to fight'.</p><div class="moreInformation"><a class="moreInformationExemples"> More </a><ul class="sentence_dictionary"><li><p class="word_origin">Debate is a word that has undergone a considerable shift in meaning. At its root is Latin <em>battere</em> ‘to fight’, and this was the original English meaning of debate. From that it acquired the sense ‘quarrel’ and ‘dispute’, which rapidly led to the more civilized idea of something deliberated and discussed. The phrase <b>debatable land</b> (Middle English) for borderland claimed by two nations, particularly the area fought over by England and Scotland, keeps the original sense.</p></li></ul></div><!--
