class="sentence_dictionary"><li><p class="word_origin">In the sense ‘flat and smooth’, even is an Old English word. Even as in <b>evening</b> (Old English) is from a different Old English word, one related to German <em>Abend</em> ‘afternoon’. <b>An even break</b>, meaning ‘a fair chance’, was popularized by the American comedian W. C. Fields (1886–1946) in his catchphrase, ‘Never give a sucker an even break’, which itself went on to become the title of one of his best-known films. There does not seem to have been a real Stephen behind the phrase <b>even Stephen</b> or <b>even Stephens</b>, meaning ‘completely even or equal’. It probably comes from <em>Journal to Stella</em> by Jonathan Swift: ‘Now we are even, quoth Stephen’ (1711). If a ship is <b>on an even keel</b> it is not tilting to the side. The keel is the supporting structure along the base of a ship; even here it is in the old sense ‘in a level position, horizontal’.</p></li></ul></div><!-- End of DIV moreInformation--></div><!-- End
