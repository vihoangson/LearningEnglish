<em>codic-</em> <span class="definition"> (see <a class="word crossRef" href="  http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/codex" title="English definition of code (codex) in Oxford Dictionaries (British &amp; World English)">codex</a>)</span>. The term originally denoted a systematic collection of statutes made by Justinian or another of the later Roman emperors; compare with <span class="definition"><a class="word crossRef" href="  http://www.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/english/code#code__10" title="English definition of code (code sense 3 of the noun) in Oxford Dictionaries (British &amp; World English)">sense 3 of the noun</a></span> (mid 18th century), the earliest modern sense.</p><div class="moreInformation"><a class="moreInformationExemples"> More </a><ul class="sentence_dictionary"><li><p class="word_origin">This was originally a term for a system of laws; the sense ‘secret writing’ developed in the early 19th century. It comes from Latin <em>codex</em>, which developed from a simple meaning of a ‘a block of wood’, to ‘a block split into leaves or tablets’ thus ‘a book’. The related term <b>codicil</b> (Late Middle English) is from Latin <em>codicillus</em>, a diminutive of <em>codex</em>, and thus applies to a ‘small’ part of a legal document.</p></li></ul></div><!--
